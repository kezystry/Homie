## 1 · Chair synthesis (the headline)

*The chair read all 13 visionaries and the surviving red-team and produced the refreshed vision.*

### Refreshed vision

Homie is "everything" at the edges and one knowing-him self at the center. The soul is two felt promises — "it knows me" and "things quietly take care of themselves" — and everything else is decoration the owner hasn't lived with yet. About 80% of the soul is already built (M0–M6 + the HA hand + the GIST integer core, 366 tests green); what remains to reach the soul is small and ships first: the morning recap, a correctable "What Homie Knows" page, one-key undo, and lights+climate autonomy. Live with that for a month and let the LIVED GAPS — not the 100-question want-map — authorize the next thing.

The architecture stays coherent because capabilities are LEAF TILES that emit facts, while the center is thin: one shared self (the GIST prose BRIEF + Remember stats), one Voice that renders facts in his current tone, one AliveState, one SpeechBudget, one EgressGuard, one AutonomyGate. Add a capability = add a tile that emits facts; you never touch the soul. That single-waist law is the anti-over-complexity invariant, enforced by a CI test (no tile speaks or egresses or acts except through its waist). The guardian is a SEPARATE deterministic reflex that runs cortex-down — protection never depends on the GPU being awake. Personality is RENDER-TIME only, downstream of every decision, so it can be bold and free precisely because it has zero authority over what Homie knows or does. Self-improvement is bold in cadence (nightly, automatic, always-latest) but disciplined at the irreversible step (signed → sandbox-tested → diff-guarded → atomic switch → auto-rollback → readable changelog), so a bad pull is a no-op that auto-reverts and can never silently widen device power or egress. The four worries — privacy leak, unreliability, nag, over-complexity — are hard guardrails engineered into mechanisms, not docstrings: one gated+logged egress chokepoint, tiers that degrade-and-announce, one global cross-tile speech budget plus an everyday owner mute, and a written Complexity Budget where every ADD must name a CUT.

### Pillars

- ONE SELF, MANY LEAVES. Capabilities are tiles that emit facts; the center is one model-of-him read through one Voice, one AliveState, one SpeechBudget, one EgressGuard, one AutonomyGate. N capabilities, 1 personality surface. Enforced by CI: no tile speaks, egresses, or acts except through its waist.
- KNOWS-ME FIRST, RELIABILITY THROUGHOUT. The first wins are the morning recap + the 'What Homie Knows' page + one-key undo — the soul made visible and correctable. Reliability is the #1 trust earner and rides under every milestone, not a phase.
- SILENT BY DEFAULT, EARNED WORDS. Homie is silent until it has earned a word; every word is true, useful, and undoable in one keystroke. A single global SpeechBudget caps owner-facing utterances across ALL tiles; overflow defers to the recap; an everyday owner mute is the fastest nag-kill.
- RENDER-TIME PERSONALITY, ZERO AUTHORITY. Tone/warmth/humor adapt freely because they are strictly downstream of decisions — they change how Homie says things, never what it knows or does. A hard obedience floor and the four nevers (no deceive/manipulate/hide/put-anyone-above-him) are signed code the persona cannot edit.
- DETERMINISTIC GUARDIAN, CORTEX-INDEPENDENT. Intrusion/hazard/emergency response is a tested deterministic state machine on the always-on floor; the LLM may narrate or de-escalate but can never be the sole authoriser of a human-calling action.
- BOLD SELF-IMPROVEMENT INSIDE STRONG RAILS. Split into free DATA drift (personality/preferences, instant-undo), gated CODE upgrade (signed → sandbox → diff-guard → atomic switch → auto-rollback → changelog), and broad-internet as untrusted DATA never as bypassable code. Boldness is safe because reverting is free and authority can never be self-granted.
- PRIVACY AS A CONTINUOUSLY-VERIFIED INVARIANT. Nothing leaves the machines without a gated, logged, owner-aware decision through ONE chokepoint; mum's flat and faceprints are unrepresentable by construction; the 'sealed' promise is generated from the very allowlist it enforces, so claim and behavior cannot drift.
- REVERSIBILITY BEATS CORRECTNESS. Every act is a selectable ledger row reversed in one keystroke; trust is measured per-capability, earned slowly, lost on a single correction; irreversible + money are permanently behind a fresh per-action yes.
- EVIDENCE AUTHORIZES SCOPE. A written Complexity Budget gates every new subsystem on 'serves knows-me-or-effortless THIS quarter AND the owner can operate it from an SSH phone session'; every ADD-council must name a CUT; the next roadmap is authored from a lived-gap log, not the want-map.

### Inventions adopted

- SpeechBudget (core/speech_budget.py) — ONE global, cross-tile cap on owner-facing utterances/day, governing the interface.say/chat.reply channel at the single Voice waist. Axis = social restraint (utterances/day), NOT GPU wakes (the M3 wake budget caps thinking, not talking). Safety/security and direct-summons exempt; the (N+1)th proactive line defers to the recap. This is the muzzle, built before any second mouth.
- The Voice (single render waist) + Coherence CI test — one prompt-template family turns any tile's emitted facts into speech in the current ToneState register; a CI assertion that NO tile calls interface.say/chat.reply directly. Makes 'one personality surface for N capabilities' a tested invariant.
- Everyday Mute / minimal-mode — a one-tap owner-only 'quiet for an hour' / 'minimal today' on the always-on floor, distinct from guest/privacy semantics, instantly reversible. The owner's own hand on a mute is the fastest nag-kill; it ships before the chatty features do.
- Recap Composer (core/recap.py) — deterministic, template-first four-beat note (Yesterday / Learned / Watching / Tidied) written even with no LLM; HARD caps enforced in code (one Learned, one Watching, honest-empty), overflow collapsed lossily ('+6 minor things, tap to see') never concatenated into a wall of text. Signed with the secret name on the intimate surface.
- 'What Homie Knows' page + memory.overlay — plain-language rows from the GIST BRIEF, each with a confidence WORD and provenance chip ('from 32 days' vs 'from 3 days'), exactly three one-tap actions ([that's me]/[not quite]/[lock]) wired to an unsigned overlay the fold reads as high-priority evidence. The trust screen + transparency promise; correction is one keypress, never a settings tree.
- The 3 GIST stat fixes folded into remember.py — FIX-1 decayed present_days so confidence is a true probability in [0,1] (kills 'empty house reads as 90% normal'); FIX-2 counted-absence so a stopped routine mean-reverts in days not a month; FIX-3 day-type {weekday/weekend/away} axis so a weekend lie-in can't poison the weekday schema. Plus the nmin evidence floor so a coincidence never renders as a fact. SNAPSHOT_VERSION 2->3 rate-preserving migration.
- Wisdom tier (core/wisdom.py) — a tiny durable store of weeks-stable preferences + pinned facts + a monthly 'what changed' digest that survives the 30d decay; consolidates on write (few KB across years). This is 'years of distilled wisdom' without raw history.
- GuardianLoop (core/guardian.py) — a deterministic, cortex-independent escalation state machine OBSERVE→ALERT→DETER→EMERGENCY with a tested per-rung evidence threshold and a two-key rule (deterministic high-confidence trigger AND failed owner-confirm) before any human is called, except life-safety hazards where seconds matter. Per-(zone,class) cooldown so a flapping sensor speaks once then summarizes.
- EgressGuard (core/egress.py) + OS egress firewall — ONE internet chokepoint, positive allowlist of (destination, purpose, payload-schema); pre-approved purposes (weather/card-prices/lookups) pass silently, everything else raises confirm via the existing Consent gate (fail-safe on timeout). Enforced at the Python seam AND a deny-by-default OS firewall. Emergency image→police/display is the one sanctioned egress, EMERGENCY-rung-only and logged.
- AutonomyGate (core/autonomy.py) + TrustLedger (core/trust.py) — a deterministic gate between proposal and Act routing silent (R2) / announce+undo (R1, transitional only, one capability at a time) / ask (R0/GUARDED); per-(capability,zone,hour-class) trust score earned on act-count+low-reversal-rate+elapsed-days, demoted on a single reversal. A static GUARDED list (deploy/guarded.toml) — money/irreversible/lock/emergency-call/send-as-him/self-modify — never auto-promotes past ask.
- confirm.response producer — a cockpit/phone Y/N source so Consent stops being a 30s fail-safe-no into the void. Prerequisite for the whole ask/GUARDED tier; without it no buy/sell or upgrade can ever actually be approved.
- Friction Ledger + full-timeline undo (M8) — every act a selectable plain-sentence row; one keypress issues the inverse act via the capability path AND records a remark-grade correction (the strongest demotion signal). Survives restart.
- Signed self-upgrade pipeline — verify owner-key signature FIRST → sandbox build+test as a separate NixOS generation → diff-guard (any touch of act-map never-touch / privacy schema / capability registry / charter / trust store / new emittable topic forces explicit owner OK even when signed) → atomic switch in the abort-gated window → post-switch health-watch with auto-rollback to a captured-good generation (not HEAD@{1}) → readable changelog line with one-tap undo. core/upgrade_guard.py is a pure, network-free, unit-testable function.
- MemoryBundle + Changelog — one canonical encrypted artifact {snapshot + GIST distillates + tile state + changelog + hashed manifest} that every backup tier (local versioned, cross-machine sync, offline cold) copies and every restore verifies-then-reads; a persistent append-only owner-readable changelog of every self-action that feeds the recap's 'Tidied' beat. A tested restore_from_bundle with a periodic restore-drill (an untested backup is not a backup).
- Tiered degradation contract — build_daemon emits system.degraded/system.recovered when the cortex link, WoL, or HA link drops; the anchor announces it ('thinking node is asleep; answering from memory'); a HomeHealth surface marks actuator/sensor state UNKNOWN (never assumed) when HA is down, replays nothing stale on reconnect. 'Told immediately' made mechanical.
- The Outbox — one persistent reviewable queue of outbound DRAFTS (listings, price changes, buys, texts, bill payments). Homie does 90% of the labor (watch/price/draft/fill) and stops at one swipe surface (Send/Edit/Skip); buying/selling/money permanently behind a fresh per-action yes. The productivity multiplier with the irreversible decisions kept human.
- VisionPerception adapter (on-Pi) — vision is just a richer PerceptionSource event vocabulary through assert_emittable; raw frames live and die on the Pi, the only egress is a pre-authorised emergency still; an honest ladder R0 presence → R1 returning-unknown (coarse label, never a vector on the wire) → R2 bodily anticipation → R3 hedged mood-band (softens environment only, never alerts) → R4 near-zero-false-alarm distress, each rung proven in CI on recorded traces before the next is built. Deception/intent stays a named horizon, not on the roadmap.
- The fixed CHARTER as signed code, persona as data — a tiny immutable module encoding the four nevers + 'no self-granted device power'; personality drifts freely on top but cannot edit the charter (it is in the diff-guarded protected set). This is how 'free personality drift' and 'never deceive' coexist.
- The secret-name primitive — an owner-set private name stored encrypted, surfaced only on intimate surfaces (recap signature, 1:1 replies), never logged / never in guest or shared context; it unlocks the warm register and gates candor, and a chosen anti-name silently triggers guest/panic posture. One word: intimacy, candor-gate, duress.
- Panic-lock vs Duress-wipe (two distinct paths) — PANIC drops keys from RAM and is password-reversible ('lock, don't lose'); DURESS is a separate secret performing an irreversible key+salt shred behind a plausible-looking normal boot. Different blast radii, heavily tested against accidental fire.
- Complexity Budget (docs/SCOPE.md) + lived-gap log — a written hard rule: no new subsystem ships unless it serves knows-me-or-effortless this quarter and the solo owner can operate it over SSH; every ADD-council names a CUT; 'lean' verdicts cannot be re-opened into 'cathedral' verdicts. The structural defense against the council ratchet that turned 'go lean' into an HMAC codec.

### Tensions resolved

- BOLD SELF-IMPROVEMENT vs SAFETY — resolved by SPLITTING self-evolution into three lanes with different blast radii, not by picking a side. Lane A DATA drift (personality/tone/preferences/proposed automations) is free with instant-undo because it is notes behind the same capability/privacy boundary everything else lives behind — it can never escalate privilege or leak. Lane B CODE upgrade is bold in cadence (nightly, automatic, always-latest) but unskippably gated at the irreversible step: signature-verified FIRST (owner key off-box) → sandbox build+test as a separate NixOS generation → diff-guard (any authority-touching change forces explicit OK even when signed) → atomic switch in the abort-gated window → post-switch health-watch auto-rollback to a captured-good generation → readable changelog with one-tap undo. Lane C broad-internet is untrusted DATA (prices/weather/advisories/hash-pinned weights) and NEVER bypassable code. The invariant tying it together: self-evolution may change Homie's MIND freely and its CODE boldly-but-gated, but can NEVER silently grant itself new DEVICE POWER or widen EGRESS — those always require the owner's yes because sent data can't be unsent and a bad authority grant the rollback can't fully undo. 'Signed-releases-only' was too weak a frame for what he wants and too strong a brake; 'gate + atomic rollback + every change narrated' gives bold self-evolution that physically cannot leave him with a broken, leaking, or over-powered brain overnight.
- EVERYTHING vs SIMPLICITY — resolved by the single-waist law plus the Complexity Budget. 'Everything' lives only at the EDGES as leaf tiles emitting facts; the CENTER is one thin coherent thing (one self, one Voice, one SpeechBudget, one AliveState, one EgressGuard, one AutonomyGate), so N capabilities add zero personality/notification/policy surfaces. A written docs/SCOPE.md gates every subsystem on 'serves knows-me-or-effortless THIS quarter AND the solo owner can operate it over SSH', every ADD-council must name a CUT, and the next roadmap is authored from a lived-gap log not the want-map. Concretely this SHELVES the GIST codec/HMAC/DP cathedral (LUKS is the at-rest story; in-Python crypto over a file that never leaves an encrypted disk buys nothing), DEFERS three-node mesh transport (run single-brain-in-practice now, keep the seam as a config flag), keeps camera human-reading at north-star not roadmap, and refuses autonomous cyber-defense block/quarantine until months of correct alerts earn the rung. The owner's 'everything' is honored as a 10-year want-map; his 'over-complexity' and 'effortlessness' guardrails outrank the feature list this quarter.
- GUARDIAN vs PRIVACY — resolved by making storage LOCAL+ENCRYPTED and egress a single GATED, LOGGED, owner-aware act. The owner explicitly wants security clips stored and emergency egress to police; the reconciliation is that clips live ONLY on the LUKS volume (drive theft = useless), never auto-egress, auto-expire on a named retention rule with logged deletions, and the one emergency image→police/display path runs through the SAME EgressGuard as everything else, EMERGENCY-rung-only and owner-confirmed-or-judgment-gated (auto only on no-response in the seconds it has). Mum's flat is unrepresentable by CONSTRUCTION — a positive zone-allowlist (front door + his entrance only), OFF-fenced at perception ingest so a mum event never even enters the model, with a test asserting no off-limits zone can produce an event, render, or clip. Faceprints/identity vectors never cross a wire (positive-schema guard, coarse labels only); the guardian is calm-by-construction (quiet-log-by-default, per-(zone,class) cooldown, two-key rule before any human is called) so 'strong guardian' and 'never a nag / never a leak' coexist.

### Guardrails (against the four worries)

- PRIVACY LEAK (worst fear): nothing leaves the machines without a gated, logged, owner-aware decision through ONE EgressGuard chokepoint, enforced at the Python seam AND a deny-by-default OS firewall; the Sealed-Network report is generated from the very allowlist it enforces (claim and behavior cannot drift); an egress-attempt ledger surfaces every allow/block in the recap; faceprints/identity vectors and mum's flat are unrepresentable by construction; the self-upgrade may never widen egress unattended.
- UNRELIABILITY / DATA-LOSS: the home is never dead — tiers degrade-and-announce instead of going silent, the always-on floor + HA keep basic functions when the cortex sleeps, state is told immediately; the nightly fold never deletes raw before the distilled artifact is durably fsync'd; one canonical encrypted MemoryBundle copied local + across-machines + offline-cold with a TESTED restore-drill; self-upgrade is atomic with automatic rollback to a captured-good generation so a bad pull can never brick the brain.
- NAG (the owner's single worst outcome): ONE global cross-tile SpeechBudget caps owner-facing utterances/day at the Voice waist (safety/summons exempt, overflow defers to the recap, never dropped-as-flood); an everyday owner Mute/minimal-mode ships before the chatty features; explain-the-unusual goes SILENT when the home is in a novel regime it can't yet baseline (nmin floor); anticipation is SILENT magic logged to the ledger, never announced; the recap is capped in code (one Learned, one Watching, honest-empty, lossy overflow); delight is rationed and OFF by default; guardian alerts are calm-by-construction with per-(zone,class) cooldowns and a measured false-alarm rate.
- OVER-COMPLEXITY: the single-waist law (one Voice/AliveState/SpeechBudget/EgressGuard/AutonomyGate, CI-enforced) + a written Complexity Budget where every ADD names a CUT and every subsystem must be operable from an SSH phone session; the GIST codec/HMAC/DP cathedral, three-node mesh transport, autonomous cyber-defense, camera intent-reading, and fine-tuning are all SHELVED until a named tripwire or lived gap authorizes them; no policy DSL — four autonomy rungs, one GUARDED TOML list, three numeric thresholds, reusing the existing capability handle/ledger/clock/histogram.
- THE FOUR NEVERS as signed code: never deceive, never manipulate, never hide what it's doing, never put anyone above him — encoded in an immutable CHARTER the personality drift cannot edit (it is in the diff-guarded protected set). Honesty is a fixed floor; warmth is the only free variable. Always honest, reads the moment.
- OBEDIENCE FLOOR + MONEY/IRREVERSIBLE GATE: for now always obey, no talking back; a hard obedience floor independent of any learned tone dial. Buying/selling/money/locks/emergency-calls/send-as-him/self-modify are permanently GUARDED — outside the auto-promotion ladder, refused at the act path without a fresh per-action yes; promotion only ever reduces how few OKs are needed, never removes the OK.

### 3.2 · Security & guardian systems (the protector): intrusion deterrence, emergency-call judgment ladder, hazard sensing, security-camera clip storage + emergency egress, entrance watching, and active network defense — all within the OFF-LIMITS line around mum's flat.

> A deterministic, cortex-independent Guardian Loop that graduates OBSERVE→ALERT→DETER→EMERGENCY with a strict two-key judgment rule before any human is called — full-deterrence on intrusion, one sanctioned emergency egress, active network defense, all sealed inside an allowlist that keeps mum's flat unrepresentable and the owner never nagged.

THE ESCALATION LADDER (deterministic, each rung needs MORE evidence than the last):
  - OBSERVE: unknown presence at a WATCHED entrance (front door / his upstairs entrance ONLY). Quiet-log it; speak only if odd (already the decided behavior in DECISIONS round-table). No action.
  - ALERT: novel/rare presence + a corroborating signal (forced-entry pattern, motion inside while armed-away, glass/door sensor). Capture a clip, notify his phone, and ASK "is this you?" with a short window.
  - DETER (full-deterrence, his explicit wish): on no-response or a confirmed-intrusion pattern — lights to full, alarm/siren, audible "you are being recorded," keep recording, and STAGE a police packet (do not send yet).
  - EMERGENCY-CALL: only on an UNMISTAKABLE trigger (verified fire/CO/smoke from a hazard sensor, or a confirmed break-in with the owner unreachable past the confirm window, or the camera's far-future "bleeding out" certainty). The rule from the interview: CONFIRM FIRST IF IT CAN; auto-act only when he doesn't respond in the seconds it has. Emergency egress (a still image / clip to police or a display) is the ONLY sanctioned off-machine data path, and it is logged and shown to him after.

The judgment threshold is two-key: a rung that calls humans (police/ambulance) requires (a) a deterministic high-confidence trigger AND (b) a failed owner-confirm, EXCEPT for life-safety hazards where seconds matter. The LLM may narrate and may RAISE the confirm bar (de-escalate a false alarm) but can never be the sole authoriser of an emergency call — that stays in tested, deterministic code, mirroring the MASTERPLAN principle that the model is untrusted and structure enforces.

CAMERA-FOR-SECURITY: clips/photos are stored locally, encrypted (LUKS floor already decided), with a retention rule and a logged discard (fits the M11 hygiene pass). Security recording is explicitly in-scope (interview round 4); the privacy red line is OFF-MACHINE egress, which is allowed ONLY in a declared emergency and ONLY to police/display. Mum's flat is unrepresentable: the camera's field of view and every watched zone is restricted to the front door + his entrance, enforced as a positive allowlist of zones, not a blocklist.

This is graduated exactly like the physical ladder: observe → alert → quarantine, with auto-quarantine reserved for unambiguous malice.

Crucially, the guardian honours all four guardrails: it is QUIET (speaks only when odd or escalating — never a nag), RELIABLE (deterministic core, runs cortex-down, tested replay scenarios), PRIVACY-SAFE (zone allowlist + single sanctioned egress path), and SIMPLE (one state machine + one rung table, no policy DSL).

### 3.3 · Privacy & sovereignty — the red lines: no-egress-without-asking, encryption + panic/duress wipe, per-person/guest privacy, camera-vs-no-leak, and making "no leak" a continuously-verified invariant rather than a docstring.

**Refined vision**

The honest reframe: the masterplan already collapsed the brain to a single always-on node, which DELETES the "faceprints cross a wire" threat the old mesh PrivacyGuard existed for (C6). So my job is NOT to harden an inter-node wire. The real, live leak surface is the INTERNET egress the owner explicitly opened — cloud AI for "anonymized hard tasks", weather/card-prices/lookups, camera-image-to-police, "text as him", DHL, and the bold "self-upgrade from the broad internet." That is where "nothing leaves without asking" is won or lost. My slice has five concrete pieces.

(1) ONE EGRESS GATE — the single chokepoint. Every byte that leaves the machines goes through one stdlib seam, core/egress.py:EgressGuard, that the daemon wires once (like build_daemon wires everything once). No tile, no cortex, no updater opens a socket directly; they request egress and the guard decides on a POSITIVE allowlist of (destination, purpose, payload-schema). Crucially it is enforced at the OS layer too (egress firewall, deny-by-default, allowlist of approved hosts), so a compromised tile that bypasses the Python seam still cannot reach the network — defense in depth, not a single Python check.

(2) PANIC + DURESS — two distinct things the interview conflated. PANIC = "lock, don't lose": one trigger (a physical combo / a secret phrase / a cockpit action) drops the LUKS key from RAM, shreds the in-memory plaintext and the scrypt-derived working keys, and the box is inert until the owner re-enters the password (password-REVERSIBLE per DECISIONS). DURESS = a SEPARATE secret that, entered under coercion, performs an IRREVERSIBLE wipe of key material + salt (data gone, not just locked) AND looks identical to a normal unlock so the coercer sees a plausible boot — the coercion guarantee the interview asked for. These are different code paths with different blast radii and must never be confused. Both are tiny: key-shred + a tmpfs scrub, gated behind a deliberate trigger so it can't fire by accident.

(3) PER-PERSON / GUEST PRIVACY (the feature he explicitly wants) — a one-tap GUEST MODE plus per-person rules. (4) CAMERA vs NO-LEAK — reconciled, not waved away. The owner WANTS security clips stored and emergency egress to police/display. The reconciliation: clips live ONLY on the encrypted volume (so theft of the drive = useless), never auto-egress, and the ONE emergency egress path (image → police/display) runs through the SAME EgressGuard with an "emergency" purpose that is owner-confirmed-or-judgment-gated exactly like the emergency-call rule (auto only if he doesn't respond in the seconds it has; else alert). Retention is rule-bound: clips auto-expire on a named retention rule, deletions logged in the dream note, and guest-mode/per-person rules can suppress a clip before it is ever written. "Security recording is in-scope" and "nothing leaves without asking" coexist because storage is local+encrypted and egress is always a gated, logged, owner-aware act.

(5) "NO LEAK" AS A CONTINUOUSLY-VERIFIED INVARIANT — the SEALED-NETWORK report (M10) generated from the SAME allowlist the guard enforces against, so the displayed promise and the runtime behavior can never drift (the exact failure that made C6's docstring a lie). The invariant is not "we wrote a guard"; it is "every blocked/allowed egress is recorded and the owner can see it."

RECONCILING AGGRESSIVE SELF-IMPROVEMENT WITH STRONG RAILS (the tension I was told to resolve, not pick a side): the owner gets his bold "always-latest, self-rewriting" home AND the security rail. (3) A SUMMONS — the owner addressed it ("Homie…"); now it's fully present and conversational. The felt rule: Homie escalates registers only on real signal (surprise, a question, an emergency) and silently de-escalates the instant the moment passes. It never lingers in a higher register — that lingering is what "naggy" feels like.

THE RECAP, AS A PRODUCT (the morning dream note, M10). Not a dashboard — a one-screen, ~5-sentence note the owner reads in 15 seconds over coffee or on his phone lock screen. Fixed four-beat shape so it's scannable without reading: **Yesterday** (one line of the day's shape: "Out 9–6, quiet evening") · **Learned** (the single most important thing it changed its mind about, named concretely) · **Watching** (at most one open uncertainty, phrased as a flag not an alarm: "an unfamiliar pattern at the side gate at 2am — flagging, not acting") · **Tidied** (M11's self-care line: what it cleaned/healed/updated overnight). Template-first so it exists every morning even if the cortex slept; the desktop rewrites it warmer only when already awake. Hard caps that protect against nagging: never more than one "Learned" and one "Watching" per note; if nothing was learned, the note says so plainly ("Nothing new — your routine held") rather than manufacturing content. Reaches him as a phone notification when away, on-screen when home (per the locked decision). The recap is where the secret/personal name lives — publicly it's "Homie," but the note is signed with the name only he chose, so the most intimate surface feels addressed to him alone.

THE TRUST PAGE, AS A PRODUCT ("What Homie Knows", M10). Two tabs, both plain-language, both read-only-plus-correct — NEVER a settings maze (over-complexity is a named fear). Tab 1 **Knows-Me**: every routine/preference it currently believes, rendered from the GIST prose BRIEF in full sentences ("On weekday mornings you usually leave around 07:08"), each row carrying a confidence WORD ("almost always / usually / sometimes") and an honest provenance chip ("from 32 days" vs "from 3 days" — firmness made visible so the owner never mistakes a guess for a fact). Every row has exactly three one-tap actions: **[that's me]** (pin), **[not quite]** (forget/correct), **[lock]** (freeze, never-relearn). Tab 2 **What-It-Did** = the Friction Ledger (M8): a reverse-chronological column of plain sentences — "6:58pm — turned on the hallway light · you turned it back off · I'll remember" — each row selectable, one keypress issues the inverse act AND teaches (remark-grade). The trust page is the physical embodiment of "it owns it" and "I'm always in control"; it is the antidote to all four fears made browsable.

OWNING MISTAKES. When Homie is corrected or reversed, it does NOT apologize repeatedly or explain at length — that's a nag wearing humility's coat. It does exactly one thing: states the lesson in the ledger row in the future tense it can keep ("· I'll remember"), once, then goes quiet and actually changes behavior on the next identical occasion. The proof of contrition is the silence next time, not the words this time.

PROACTIVE = EXPLAIN-THE-UNUSUAL ONLY. Routine is silent by construction. Homie speaks proactively in exactly two cases: something is wrong (security/welfare/hazard), or it's about to do something new and wants a yes (autonomy-ladder: "want me to warm the lights at 7?"). On a yes it does it automatically thereafter and never asks again — asking twice for the same earned thing is the cardinal nag sin. Everything else routes to the morning recap, batched, never interrupting.

THE ADAPTIVE PERSONALITY DIAL. One internal scalar I call the **register dial** (terse↔warm), learned from his reactions, never a settings slider he has to tune. Default terse-and-factual; it earns warmth and small delights (occasional thoughtful touches) only after reliability is established and only when his pattern shows he wants them. "A little character now, a real character eventually" maps to: the dial's warm end widens over months, but the obedience floor (always do what he says, no talking back) is hard-wired and never drifts.

**Inventions**

### 3.6 · Autonomy & safety — the earned-autonomy ladder, trust measurement, undo, guest mode, and the full off switch

> Autonomy is a per-capability ladder Homie CLIMBS by measured evidence (act count, low reversal rate, days) and FALLS fast on a single correction — lights+climate first, irreversible+money pinned forever at ask, all of it transparent, undoable on one key, freezable by guest mode, and killable by an always-on off switch the model can never touch.

**Refined vision**

Autonomy in Homie is a LADDER each capability climbs by earning evidence, never a global "smart enough" flag. The owner's wishes map to four standing rungs per capability:

- R0 ASK — every action proposes and waits for a yes (the existing Consent gate). Default for anything new.
- R1 ANNOUNCE-THEN-ACT — Homie acts but says what it did, with a one-key undo in the window ("warmed the kitchen at 7 — undo?"). The light/climate comfort layer starts here once a routine is confident.
- R2 SILENT — acts without narration; only surfaces in the morning note. Reserved for lights+climate after a long clean record.
- GUARDED — a permanent ceiling that NEVER auto-promotes past R0/announce: anything irreversible or spending money (buy/sell on KartenWerk, locks/garage, emergency calls, sending a message as him, deleting data). These always ask, forever, by policy — promotion only moves how few OKs are needed, never removes the OK.

Crucially this reuses what exists rather than inventing a parallel system. The act-map `never_touch` + the M5 CapabilityRegistry are already the hard floor (a tile can only drive what its manifest declares, at its declared priority). I add a thin AUTONOMY GATE that sits between a tile/Reason proposal and Act: it looks up the capability's current rung and either lets it through silently (R2), wraps it in an announce+undo (R1), or routes it to Consent (R0/GUARDED). The model is never trusted to set its own rung — the gate is deterministic core code reading a persisted ladder state, exactly as the capability handle is minted by trusted core, not the tile.

TRUST is measured, not asserted, with a simple auditable score per (capability, zone, hour-class) — the same grain as the pattern-of-life histogram so it can't drift from what Homie actually learned. A capability earns promotion R0→R1→R2 when, over a rolling window: (a) it has acted/proposed at least N times in that context, (b) the human-reversal rate (an undo or a remark within the friction window) stays under a low threshold, and (c) a minimum number of calendar days have elapsed (the M2.5 clock; reuses `Expectation.days` from the second-review brainstorm #5). A single reversal at R2 DEMOTES one rung and resets the day counter — trust is slow to earn, fast to lose. GUARDED capabilities are excluded from auto-promotion by a static policy list, no matter how clean the record. Every promotion/demotion is one plain line in the timeline ("kitchen lighting earned silent mode — 3 weeks, no corrections") so the ladder is fully transparent, which is itself the anti-deception guarantee.

UNDO is the trust spine (MASTERPLAN principle 7: reversibility beats correctness). Full-timeline undo means every act Homie took is a selectable row that issues the inverse act via the M5 capability path AND records a remark-grade correction — so undoing both reverses the world and demotes/teaches in one keystroke. This is the M8 Friction Ledger, which I own the safety semantics of: an undo is the strongest trust signal, weighted heaviest in the score.

GUEST MODE and the OFF SWITCH are two master controls layered above the ladder. Guest mode is one tap: it freezes every capability to R0 (nothing acts silently while a guest is present), suppresses proactive narration, and — tying to the privacy feature the owner asked for — stops learning from anyone but the owner (per-person privacy). The OFF switch is a true kill: it stops the autonomy gate from authorizing ANY act (the home falls back to plain HA routines + manual control), is reachable even when the cortex/desktop is asleep (it lives in the always-on floor, not the GPU node), and is logged with who/when. Neither control can be set BY Homie — both are owner-only, and the off switch fails toward "off" (a gate that can't read its state refuses to authorize).

Reconciling the owner's AMBITIOUS self-evolution with strong rails (the explicit tension): self-modification is just another capability on the ladder, permanently pinned at GUARDED with extra gates. Aggressive ("always latest") is honored by Homie freely PROPOSING and PREPARING upgrades nightly — fetch the vetted channel, build the new NixOS closure, run the full suite + boot/health smoke gate in the M11 maintenance window — but the SWITCH to the new generation is authorized only when: signed/pinned source verified, smoke gate green, abort gates clear (nobody home, no live security event, not gaming), AND the change is reversible by atomic NixOS rollback. Bold in preparation, gated at the irreversible step, always rolling-back-capable. Personality/code drift is allowed freely because it's cheap-reversible (rollback + the undo timeline); buying, sending-as-him, and unlocking are never auto, because they aren't.

**Inventions**

- AutonomyGate (new core/autonomy.py): a deterministic core component between proposal and Act that reads each capability's current rung and routes silent (R2) / announce+undo (R1) / Consent (R0/GUARDED). Trusted-core only, never the model; reuses the M5 CapabilityRegistry handle to identify the capability.
- TrustLedger (new core/trust.py): per-(capability, zone, hour-class) rolling score from three measured signals — act count, human-reversal rate (undo/remark inside the friction window), and elapsed-days. Persisted, replay-deterministic, same grain as the pattern-of-life histogram. Emits trust.promoted / trust.demoted events.
- A static GUARDED policy list (deploy/guarded.toml): the capabilities that NEVER auto-promote past ask — irreversible + money + lock/garage + emergency-call + send-as-him + delete-data + self-modify. Loaded once; the AutonomyGate consults it before any promotion. Friction is the feature.
- The promotion/demotion rules as explicit thresholds (N acts, max reversal rate, min days; single-reversal-demotes-and-resets) — written down, tuned per rung, surfaced in the morning note so the owner sees exactly why something earned or lost autonomy.
- The confirm.response PRODUCER that closes the N10 dead-end: a cockpit/phone Y/N (and later nod/shake) source so Consent isn't a 30s fail-safe-no into the void. Without it the whole R0/GUARDED tier can only ever time out to 'no'.
- Announce-with-undo wrapper: an R1 act emits one interface.say/chat.reply line plus an undo-armed timer (M2.5 clock) so the inverse act is one keypress for a bounded window — the felt 'I'm in control' moment at the comfort layer.
- Guest-mode and Off-switch as bus-level master overrides the AutonomyGate honors first (guest → clamp all to R0 + stop non-owner learning; off → authorize nothing), reachable from the always-on floor independent of the sleeping GPU cortex, owner-set-only, fail-toward-safe.
- Self-evolution as a GUARDED capability with an extra irreversibility gate: bold nightly PREPARE-and-smoke-test, gated atomic SWITCH (signed source + green gate + abort gates + NixOS rollback). This is the concrete reconciliation of 'always latest' with 'signed/human-gated'.

**Risks**

RISK 1 — Auto-promotion mislearns and a silent act surprises/annoys him (becomes the NAG/over-reach he fears). Mitigate: slow-up/fast-down asymmetry (one reversal demotes + resets days), conservative thresholds, GUARDED list is a hard ceiling, and R1's announce+undo always precedes R2 so nothing goes fully silent before he's seen and accepted it announced. The morning note shows pending promotions before they happen ("kitchen is close to earning silent mode — ok?").

RISK 2 — The autonomy gate is bypassable (a tile or the model acts without going through the rung check), making the ladder cosmetic. Mitigate: the gate sits at the SAME consumer chokepoint as M5's capability check inside Act — there is one act path (build_daemon, one graph), and the AutonomyGate is wired unconditionally there; a contract test asserts no act reaches the home without a rung decision, the mirror of test_forged_safety_emit_refused.

RISK 3 — Self-evolution rail is the genuinely dangerous power: auto-pulling and switching to new code on the home's brain. Mitigate: irreversible-step gating (signed/pinned source, full-suite + boot smoke gate, atomic NixOS switch with every prior generation a bootable rollback), abort gates (occupied/secure/gaming), and self-modify pinned GUARDED so personality/prompt drift is cheap-reversible but the closure switch is the one thing that must pass all gates. Never unattended pull-and-run.

RISK 4 — Off switch / guest mode that depend on the cortex are useless exactly when the cortex is wedged or compromised. Mitigate: both live in the always-on floor (mini-PC/Pi), independent of the GPU node, and fail toward safe — a gate that can't read its own master-control state authorizes nothing (off) and clamps to R0 (guest).

RISK 5 — Consent is a 30s timeout into nothing (N10): every GUARDED/R0 action silently fails-safe-no, so the ask tier feels broken and the owner can never actually approve a buy/sell or an upgrade from his phone. Mitigate: ship the confirm.response producer (cockpit/phone Y/N) as a prerequisite for the ladder, not an afterthought; fail-safe-no stays, but now a real yes is possible.

RISK 6 — Over-complexity (his explicit fear): a big policy DSL / per-capability UI sprawl. Mitigate: NO policy language — four rungs, one GUARDED TOML list, three numeric thresholds, reusing the existing capability handle, friction ledger, clock, and histogram grain. The ladder is ~two small stdlib modules + tests, not a subsystem.

**Roadmap contribution**

### 3.8 · Reliability & resilience — "the home is never dead": tiered survival, layered backups, self-heal + rollback + changelog

> Make "the home is never dead" provable: tiers that degrade-and-announce instead of going silent, one encrypted memory bundle copied local + across-machines + offline-cold, and bold self-upgrade made safe by atomic NixOS switches with automatic rollback and a changelog that owns every move out loud.

**Refined vision**

Reliability is the owner's #1 trust earner, so it must be a FELT, provable property, not a docstring. Concretely Homie should guarantee three things, each visible: (1) it never goes silent, (2) it never loses what it knows, and (3) it can always undo a bad change to itself.

The good news: the spine already exists and is honest. The bus has a crash-safe DurabilityLog (atomic snapshot via os.replace + fsync, ordered rotate→commit→delete so a crash can't corrupt or lose committed state), the Supervisor quarantines/reloads faulting tiles in-process, ritual.consolidate() self-heals QUARANTINED/DEGRADED tiles behind abort gates and emits an advisory restart_decision, and scripts/update.py is a conservative pull→full-suite-health-check→restart channel with a one-line `git reset --hard HEAD@{1}` rollback. My job is to (a) make survival span the three machines the owner re-confirmed (Pi camera-edge + always-on mini-PC anchor + on-demand GPU desktop), (b) turn the layered-backup wish into real local + across-machine + offline-cold copies, and (c) turn the self-upgrade channel into the atomic, auto-rollback NixOS generation switch the M11 vision needs — reconciling 'always latest / self-rewriting' with 'strong rails' by making rollback the thing that makes boldness safe.

The survival model, made concrete:
- TIER 0 (always works, no brain): HA on the mini-PC owns deterministic device routines (door, lights, alarm). If Homie's brain crashes AND the desktop is asleep, the home still locks, lights, and alarms because those live in HA, not Homie. This is the floor: 'basic functions survive even if Homie is fully down.'
- TIER 1 (anchor alive, cortex asleep): the mini-PC runs the full build_daemon graph + Remember + AnchorVoice. M0 already guarantees a typed line never vanishes; I extend that to a HEARTBEAT — the anchor is the watchdog of last resort. The owner is TOLD immediately ('thinking node is asleep; I'm answering from memory').
- TIER 2 (full brain): desktop woken on novelty for heavy reasoning, then sleeps. A WoL failure or a desktop that's in Windows degrades gracefully to Tier 1, never to silence.
The contract: each tier degrades to the one below it AUTOMATICALLY and announces the degradation. 'Told immediately' is a hard requirement from the interview (round 12).

Backups, made concrete (honoring 'encrypted local + across-machines + offline cold'):
- LOCAL: the snapshot+segments already give crash recovery on one disk. Add a versioned, rotated encrypted backup of {snapshot, GIST distillates, tile state, changelog} so disk-failure ≠ total loss.
- ACROSS-MACHINES: nightly, the mini-PC pushes its encrypted memory bundle to the desktop and vice-versa over the private network (LUKS-at-rest on both, so a stolen drive is useless — the interview's chosen baseline). Two machines must die to lose memory.
- OFFLINE COLD: a periodic encrypted export to a removable drive the owner rotates (the 3-2-1 cold copy). Deferred-but-designed; the bundle format is the same.
The unifying invention: ONE canonical 'memory bundle' artifact (snapshot + GIST + tile state + changelog + a manifest hash) that every backup tier copies and every restore reads, so local/across/cold are three destinations for one verified thing — no divergent formats, no over-complexity.

Self-heal + rollback + changelog, made concrete:
- A persistent, append-only CHANGELOG is the spine of trust. Every self-action — tile quarantine/reload, memory prune (what it forgot, with the retention rule), code upgrade (old gen → new gen), restart, and any auto-rollback — writes one plain line. The morning dream note (M10) reads from it. 'It owns its mistakes out loud' becomes literally true.
- Self-upgrade becomes a NixOS generation switch: build the new closure in the maintenance window, switch atomically ONLY if it builds AND passes the smoke gate (full suite + boot/health probe), else stay put. Every prior generation is a bootable rollback, so a bad self-rewrite cannot brick the always-on brain. This is exactly what reconciles the owner's 'bold, always-latest, may rewrite its own code' with the security reviewer's 'human-gated': the gate is the smoke test + atomic rollback, not a human in the loop for every change — boldness is SAFE because reverting is free and automatic. A post-switch HEALTH WATCH window (N minutes of green health beats) is the auto-rollback trigger: if the new generation regresses health, the OS rolls back to the last-known-good generation and logs it. Aggressive forward, instant reverse.

The four guardrails this domain owns: never UNRELIABLE/data-loss is my whole charter; I actively defend OVER-COMPLEXITY by reusing the existing crash-safe primitives and the one-bundle/one-changelog discipline rather than inventing a backup framework; I stay out of NAG territory (reliability work is silent — it only speaks the morning changelog line and the degradation notices the owner explicitly asked for); PRIVACY is honored because every backup is LUKS-encrypted and the bundle obeys the same positive-schema guard (no raw faces/audio leave a machine even in a backup).

**Inventions**

- MemoryBundle: one canonical, versioned, encrypted artifact = {snapshot.json + GIST distillates + tile state + changelog + manifest with content hashes}. The single unit every backup tier copies and every restore verifies-then-reads. Kills format divergence by construction.
- A persistent append-only Changelog (core/changelog.py): every self-action (heal, prune, upgrade old-gen→new-gen, restart, auto-rollback) writes one plain owner-readable line with a reason. Source of the morning dream-note's 'what I cleaned/fixed/updated' and the audit trail that makes self-rewriting trustworthy.
- Tiered degradation contract + announcement: build_daemon emits a system.degraded / system.recovered event when a tier falls (cortex unreachable, WoL failed, HA down). The anchor announces it; the owner is 'told immediately' as the interview demands. A SurvivalLedger records uptime per tier so 'never dead' is a measured number, not a slogan.
- Heartbeat watchdog: the always-on anchor is the watchdog of last resort. A liveness beat per subsystem (HA link, cortex link, perception); a missed beat trips a heal attempt then a degradation announcement. Reuses the Supervisor's quarantine/reload — no new framework.
- Cross-machine backup sync: nightly encrypted MemoryBundle push mini-PC↔desktop over the private network (rsync-of-one-file, stdlib + ssh/scp, no backup daemon). Two machines must die to lose memory.
- NixOS atomic self-upgrade with a post-switch HEALTH WATCH auto-rollback: build new closure → smoke gate (suite + boot/health probe) → atomic switch → watch N green health beats → auto-rollback to last-good generation on regression, every step changelogged. This is the mechanism that makes 'bold always-latest self-rewrite' safe.
- Restore drill / recovery test: a tested `restore_from_bundle` path + a periodic self-test that restores a bundle into a scratch dir and verifies the model reconstructs bit-identically. An untested backup is not a backup — this proves it.
- Offline cold-copy export: a single command (and a scheduled prompt) that writes the encrypted MemoryBundle to a removable drive, completing 3-2-1. Format-identical to the other tiers; deferred but designed now.

**Risks**

"1) Self-upgrade that runs code from the broad internet on the home's brain is the single most dangerous power in the whole system (the owner wants it bold; the security reviewer wants it gated). MITIGATE: never auto-pull-and-run without (a) a pinned/signed source (signed git tag or pinned flake rev — provenance, not arbitrary HEAD), (b) the full test-suite + boot/health smoke gate before any switch, (c) atomic NixOS generation switch so the running system is never half-upgraded, (d) a post-switch health-watch window with AUTOMATIC rollback to the last-good generation, (e) every step changelogged. Boldness lives in the cadence (nightly, unattended); safety lives in the rollback being free and automatic. Never auto-grant new DEVICE authority in an upgrade without the owner's yes — capability grants stay human-gated even when code does not. 2) The 'silent backup that never restores' classic failure: an encrypted bundle nobody ever restored is a false promise. MITIGATE: ship `restore_from_bundle` WITH a periodic restore-drill self-test that reconstructs into scratch and asserts bit-identical — fail loud in the morning note if it ever breaks. 3) Split-brain / stale-restore: if two machines hold diverging bundles and a restore picks the wrong one, you silently roll memory back days. MITIGATE: the manifest carries a monotonic generation + content hash; restore always picks the highest verified generation and logs which it chose. 4) The cure being worse than the disease (crash-loop on a bad heal): an unrecoverable tile that reloads forever can take down the always-on brain. MITIGATE: bounded heal attempts → leave QUARANTINED and SURFACE in the morning note rather than crash-loop (the daemon stays up minus one tile; reliability of the whole beats perfection of the part). 5) Over-complexity creep — building a 'backup framework' or a distributed-consensus layer. MITIGATE: hold the line at one bundle, one changelog, rsync-of-a-file, and stdlib; no new daemons, no vector store, no consensus protocol. The owner explicitly fears over-complexity; the discipline is the feature. 6) Maintenance disrupting a live home. MITIGATE: reuse RitualGates — no upgrade/restart while someone's home, a security event is live, or the desktop is gaming; consolidation (invisible) still runs, only the disruptive tail is fenced."

**Roadmap contribution**

- R0 (slots beside M8/M11 prep): core/changelog.py — a persistent append-only owner-readable log of every self-action. Wire heal/prune/restart to write to it. Acceptance: a seeded heal+prune writes legible lines that survive a restart. This is the cheap spine everything else narrates into.
- R1 (with M10): tiered degradation announcement — emit system.degraded/system.recovered when the cortex link, WoL, or HA link drops/returns; the anchor speaks it; a SurvivalLedger records per-tier uptime. Acceptance: simulating a cortex-unreachable scenario yields one degradation notice + a recovery notice, and the owner is told (never silent). Delivers the interview's 'told immediately'.
- R2 (with M11 hygiene): define the canonical MemoryBundle {snapshot + GIST + tile state + changelog + hashed manifest} and a tested restore_from_bundle that reconstructs bit-identically. Acceptance: backup→wipe-scratch→restore reproduces the model exactly; a restore-drill self-test runs in the nightly pass and reports green/red in the morning note.
- R3 (with M11 backups): local versioned encrypted backup + nightly cross-machine bundle sync (mini-PC↔desktop over the private net, LUKS-at-rest). Acceptance: after one machine's state dir is destroyed, the home restores its memory from the peer's latest verified bundle; generation+hash picks the newest, logged.
- R4 (the M11 upgrade core — panel-decided first, per CLAUDE.md): NixOS atomic self-upgrade with smoke gate + post-switch health-watch auto-rollback, from a pinned/signed channel, fully changelogged, RitualGates-fenced. Acceptance: a failing build/smoke-gate leaves the running generation untouched; a passing one switches atomically; a post-switch health regression auto-rolls-back to the last-good generation and writes the changelog line. This is where 'bold always-latest' and 'strong rails' are reconciled.

### 3.9 · Self-evolution & safe auto-update — the rails that make aggressive self-improvement safe

> Split self-evolution into three lanes — free DATA drift (personality, already safe behind the capability/privacy boundary + a signed charter), bold-but-gated CODE upgrade (signed → sandbox-tested → diff-guarded → atomic switch with auto-rollback → readable changelog), and broad-internet as untrusted DATA never as bypassable code — so Homie improves itself aggressively every night yet can never silently grant itself new device power, leak, deceive, or brick the always-on brain.

**Refined vision**

The owner's "bold, always-latest, self-rewriting, broad-internet, free-drift" wish and the reviewer's "signed/gated" demand are not actually in conflict once you SPLIT self-evolution into THREE lanes with different blast radius and different rails — instead of one scary "it rewrites itself" knob:

LANE A — DATA-PLANE DRIFT (no rails needed; this is most of what "feels like" self-improvement). Personality/tone, learned suppressions, retrieval episodes, the secret name, calibrated thresholds, proposed automations. These are NOTES THE LLM READS and rows in state, never executable code or new authority. The architecture already makes this safe: model stays untrusted, tiles + capability gate + act-map enforce, identity vectors never cross the wire. So personality "freely adapts to him" with ZERO gate — it can never escalate privilege, power a new device, or leak, because it is data behind the same boundaries everything else lives behind. This is the lane the owner emotionally means by "freely adapt," and it's already free. We only add: every drift is journaled and one-tap revertible (ties to the Friction Ledger / "what it knows" page), and tone drift is bounded by a fixed CHARTER (the four nevers: no deceive/manipulate/hide/put-anyone-above-him) that personality is not allowed to edit — the charter is signed code, the persona is data on top of it.

LANE B — CODE SELF-UPGRADE (the real authority grant; this is where the rails live). New code can rewrite the act-map, disable the privacy guard, exfiltrate the log — running new code IS the largest power grant in the system, larger than any device action (NEW-3's key insight). So "bold/always-latest" here means a fast, fully-automatic PIPELINE, not an absence of checks. The rail is a five-gate conveyor where EVERY gate is automatic so it feels bold, but the chain is unskippable: (1) SIGNATURE — the pulled ref must be a tag/commit signed by the owner's key (or a Homie self-signing key the owner provisioned), verified BEFORE any pulled code is imported or executed; an unsigned/badly-signed ref is rejected and never runs. This kills NEW-3: a branch-pusher or supply-chain compromise can ship green tests but not the owner's signature. (2) SANDBOX BUILD + TEST — build the candidate in an isolated worktree/closure (on NixOS, a separate generation built but NOT switched) with no creds and no device access, run the FULL suite + a boot/health smoke probe there, never against live state. (3) DIFF GUARD — a structural check that the diff didn't touch the protected set (act-map "never-touch" entries, the privacy schema/allowlist, the capability registry, the charter, the signing-key trust store) without an extra explicit owner OK; touching authority surfaces is the one thing that escalates from auto to ask. (4) ATOMIC SWITCH + AUTO-ROLLBACK — switch only in the M11 abort-gated window (nobody home / not gaming / no live security), behind a post-switch health watchdog: if the new generation fails to come up healthy within a deadline, auto-roll-back to the captured-good generation/commit (NOT HEAD@{1} — NEW-3's reflog-fragility fix). NixOS makes this uniquely safe: every prior generation is a bootable rollback, switch is atomic, a bad upgrade can't brick the always-on brain. (5) READABLE CHANGELOG — the morning dream-note names in plain words what changed, what it cleaned/healed/upgraded, and the old→new version, with one-tap "undo this upgrade." So: aggressive (fully automatic, nightly, always-latest) AND safe (signed → sandboxed → diff-guarded → atomically rolled back → narrated).

LANE C — BROAD-INTERNET UPDATES (the riskiest "source"; treat the internet as untrusted INPUT, never as a trusted CODE source). "Updates from the broad internet with checks" splits cleanly: broad internet is fine as a source of DATA (card prices, weather, security advisories, model weights by hash) but NEVER as a source of unsigned EXECUTABLE that bypasses Lane B. So a broad-internet code suggestion becomes a Lane-B candidate the owner's key must still sign; broad-internet data flows through the existing online-consent posture (pre-approved trivia direct, sensitive via VPN/Tor, skeptical+cited, nothing personal leaves). Model-weight updates are pinned-by-hash and A/B'd against the incumbent before promotion (reuses the M6 model-card A/B discipline).

THE INVARIANT THAT TIES IT TOGETHER, honoring the owner's explicit fear: self-evolution can change Homie's MIND freely (Lane A) and its CODE boldly-but-gated (Lane B), but it can NEVER, by any self-upgrade path, silently grant itself new DEVICE POWER. New authority (a new actuator, the lock, spending, a new emittable topic) is the protected set in gate (3) and always requires the owner's explicit yes — exactly the autonomy-ladder rule, now enforced at the upgrade layer too.

**Inventions**

- Signature-verified update channel: extend selfupdate.decide() to require an owner-key-signed git tag/commit (git verify-tag / verify-commit, or minisign over the closure hash) verified BEFORE the candidate is built or imported; rejection is loud and the running daemon is untouched. A small `core/trust.py` holds the owner's allowed-signer set; the trust store itself is in the diff-guarded protected set (you can't sign away the signing rule).
- Captured-good rollback anchor: replace the fragile `git reset --hard HEAD@{1}` with a recorded last-known-good (commit hash + NixOS generation number) persisted before each switch, so rollback targets the proven generation even if the reflog moved (NEW-3 second half). On NixOS this is just the generation int.
- Three-lane manifest + diff guard (`core/upgrade_guard.py`): classify every candidate change as data-drift (Lane A, auto), ordinary-code (Lane B, signed+tested+auto-switch), or authority-touching (act-map never-touch / privacy schema / capability registry / charter / trust store / new emittable topic) which forces an explicit owner OK. Pure function over a diff + the protected-path set, fully unit-testable with no network.
- Sandboxed candidate evaluator: build+test the candidate as a separate NixOS generation (built, not switched) or an isolated worktree with no secrets/device sockets mounted, run the full suite + a boot/health smoke probe there. The test-gate becomes meaningful (it gates accidental breakage) AFTER the signature gate has already handled malice — order matters: verify, THEN trust the tests.
- Post-switch health watchdog with auto-revert: a deadline-bounded boot/health probe after the atomic switch; on miss, auto-roll-back to the captured-good generation and write a failure line to the morning note. Reuses ritual's RitualGates so a switch only happens in the nobody-home/not-gaming/no-live-security window.
- The fixed CHARTER as signed code, persona as data: a tiny immutable charter module encoding the four nevers (no deceive/manipulate/hide/put-anyone-above-him) + 'no self-granted device power'; personality/tone is a data overlay layered on top and may drift freely but cannot edit the charter (charter is in the protected set). This is how 'free personality drift' and 'never deceive' coexist.
- Upgrade changelog → dream-note surface: a structured, human-readable record (old→new ref, what changed, lane, tests passed, signed-by) folded into the M10/M11 morning note with one-tap 'undo this upgrade' (revert to captured-good). Makes every self-change visible and reversible — the owner's transparency + reversibility rules at the upgrade layer.
- Model-weight A/B promotion by hash: broad-internet model updates pinned by content hash, evaluated against the incumbent on a held-out prompt set (reuses M6's golden-prompt harness) before promotion; abliteration/tool-calling regressions block promotion automatically.

**Risks**

"R1 — Self-blessing tests (NEW-3, the headline). A test-gate alone is theatre: whoever controls the code controls the tests. MITIGATION: signature gate runs FIRST and independently; the test-gate only ever certifies that signed code didn't accidentally break — never that unsigned code is safe. The owner's key lives off the box (on his phone / a hardware token) so a box compromise can't sign new code. R2 — Key compromise / lost key. If the owner's signing key leaks, the whole chain falls; if he loses it, the box can never update. MITIGATION: a small allowed-signer set (owner key + a backup) in the diff-guarded trust store; key rotation is itself an authority-touching change requiring the OLD key; an offline cold backup of the trust config (matches the layered-backup decision). R3 — A bad upgrade lands while he's away and bricks the always-on brain. MITIGATION: keep the pure decision logic stdlib + unit-testable (as selfupdate.py already is), lean on NixOS's BUILT-IN atomic rollback rather than a bespoke rollback engine, and ship it as an extension of the existing update.py path — not a new subsystem. If a piece doesn't earn its place against a named threat, cut it. R6 — Personality drift becomes manipulative or sycophantic over time (drifts toward telling him what he wants). MITIGATION: the charter (no deceive/manipulate, always honest) is signed code drift can't edit; drift is journaled and revertible; honesty is a fixed floor, warmth is the only free variable. R7 — Rollback loses learned memory/data (data-loss is a named guardrail). MITIGATION: code rollback and memory are separate stores; an upgrade switch never touches the encrypted pattern-of-life/episode store; the captured-good anchor is code+generation only, and the nightly tidy never deletes data without a named retention rule + logged line (M11 anti-goal already)."

**Roadmap contribution**

### 3.10 · Vision & human-reading — on-device camera understanding from presence → mood → distress → intent

> Vision is an honest ladder of independently-valuable rungs — presence → returning-unknown → bodily anticipation → gentle mood → a near-zero-false-alarm distress ladder — all on-Pi, raw frames never crossing except one pre-authorised emergency still, each rung just a typed PerceptionSource event proven in CI before the next is built.

**Refined vision**

The honest ladder. Today the "camera" is a fiction: `perception/` is an empty README, security flags presence by rate alone (`tiles/security/handlers.py`), and the Pi-as-camera node is decided but unbuilt. The owner's north star is "read people" (posture→mood→distress→intent→deception, years out) valued *mostly for understanding HIM*, with judgment-based emergency calls and mum's flat permanently OFF-LIMITS. The way to honour both that ambition and his four fears (privacy leak, unreliability, nag, over-complexity) is to make every rung INDEPENDENTLY VALUABLE and to never ship a higher rung until the one below it is reliable. The whole stack runs at the edge on the Pi+Hailo; only normalized events cross to the brain; raw frames NEVER leave the Pi except one path — an emergency still/clip the owner pre-authorised, egressed only on an unmistakable trigger.

The discipline that makes this safe is the spine the codebase already enforces: vision is a `PerceptionSource` (`core/perceive.py`) that emits typed events through `assert_emittable`, and tiles consume those events exactly like presence does today. No new wiring class — vision is just a richer event vocabulary on the existing intake seam. This means each rung is testable through `SyntheticPerception` (M2) with recorded event traces, NO camera and NO GPU needed in CI — the same proof-discipline as the rest of Homie.

The rungs (each ships value, each gated on the prior being reliable):

R0 — Honest presence at watched entrances (front door + his upstairs entrance ONLY). Person/no-person + zone + a coarse familiar/unfamiliar bit, derived on-Pi from Frigate-style detection. This already has a consumer (Security tile) and a felt moment (quiet-log-the-unknown, speak-only-if-odd, per DECISIONS M7). Value: the guardian floor. NO mood, NO identity vectors on the wire.

R1 — Returning-unknown faceprint, on-Pi only. A short-lived encrypted vector held in the Pi's FOV (already in SECURITY.md §4), enabling "the same unfamiliar person came back twice this week" WITHOUT ever meshing or storing a photo. The faceprint is a coarse household/known/guest/unknown *label* by the time it reaches the bus (M10 trust tiers). Value: stranger-by-recurrence; the security pillar's real teeth. This is the privacy red-line stress test — it must be impossible by construction (positive-schema guard, M7) for the vector to cross.

R2 — Presence as a richer signal for KNOWING HIM (the soul). On-Pi pose/activity classification (sitting/standing/moving/absent, rough activity tempo) becomes events that feed the pattern-of-life — so the morning recap and anticipation get sharper ("you usually settle at the desk ~09:00") WITHOUT any affect claim. This is the rung that serves "understanding him" directly and is the highest-value/lowest-risk affect-adjacent step. Value: anticipation gets bodily, not just door-timed.

R3 — Coarse affect/wellbeing signal (mood), heavily hedged. On-Pi, low-resolution, NEVER a hard claim — a slow-moving "energy/tension" estimate that only ever gently adapts the environment (calmer lights when winding down, fewer interruptions when tense — exactly the interview's ask, round 3) and is shown to him as a correctable belief on the "what it knows" page. It is advisory, never an alert, never logged as raw, never sent anywhere. Value: the home reads his state and softens around it.

R4 — Distress/medical judgment ladder (the years-deep rung). On-Pi detection of unmistakable distress patterns (a fall + sustained no-recovery, a person down). Strict ladder: AMBIGUOUS → alert HIM only; UNMISTAKABLE + he doesn't respond in the seconds it has → the camera may send a still to emergency services / a display (the one pre-authorised raw-egress path, interview round 13). Welfare-shaped for mum at her front door ONLY (notice-if-rhythm-off → flag to him; clear emergencies → act), strictly within her hard limits — front door + his entrance, never her flat. Value: the guardian's highest stakes, gated to near-zero false alarms.

Deception cues / intent-reading stay an explicitly named horizon (R5+), NOT built, NOT promised on a date — the interview frames them as "years out" and "only if flawless." Honesty about what is NOT built is itself a feature here.

Cross-cutting invariants for the whole domain: (1) raw frames live and die on the Pi; (2) the ONLY raw egress is an emergency still on an unmistakable trigger he pre-authorised; (3) every vision capability is a `PerceptionSource` event passing `assert_emittable` — so the existing privacy floor covers it; (4) mum's flat is unrepresentable by absence (no glyph, no zone) — the OFF-fence in GIST and at ingest; (5) per-person/guest privacy (one-tap "don't record while X is around", interview round 18) is honoured at the Pi, before any event is emitted; (6) every rung is replayable in CI via recorded normalized-event traces — reliability is proven, not asserted.

**Inventions**

- VisionPerception adapter (perception/ on the Pi): wraps Frigate/Hailo detections and emits ONLY normalized events (zone, person/no-person, familiar bit, pose-class, optional affect-band) through the existing PerceptionSource seam. The single place raw frames exist; nothing else in the codebase ever sees a pixel.
- A vision event vocabulary + its positive-schema declaration: presence.* (exists), vision.pose, vision.activity, vision.affect_band, vision.distress, vision.unknown_return — each with a DECLARED field set/types/bounds so the M7 positive-schema guard makes a faceprint/embedding/crop crossing impossible by construction, regardless of key name or nesting (closes the C6 leak vector for vision specifically).
- On-Pi returning-unknown faceprint store: a short-lived, encrypted, vector-ONLY faceprint that never leaves the Pi and is reduced to a coarse {household|known|guest|unknown} label before any bus event — so identity vectors never touch bus or mesh (the M10 trust-tier contract, sourced at the camera).
- The distress/medical JUDGMENT LADDER as testable code: a state machine AMBIGUOUS→alert-him / UNMISTAKABLE+no-response→emergency-egress, with the emergency raw-still path as the single audited exception to no-raw-egress, gated behind an owner pre-authorisation + a confirm-or-auto-after-N-seconds (reuses the Consent confirm path, M8).
- Per-person / guest privacy gate AT THE PI: a one-tap 'don't record/represent while person X is present' that suppresses event emission at the source (before assert_emittable), not after — the privacy feature he explicitly named (round 18), enforced where the frames actually are.
- A synthetic vision-event scenario library (extends tests/scenarios/): recorded normalized traces — unknown_return_twice, fall_then_no_recovery, ambiguous_slump, calm_wind_down, mum_rhythm_off — so every rung (including the affect and distress ladders) is deterministically replayable through the real build_daemon graph with NO camera, NO Hailo, NO GPU in CI. This is what makes 'reliability' provable for the riskiest features.
- An on-Pi affect estimator that emits a BAND not a number, with a calibrated abstain ('unknown') default — designed to be wrong-safely: it can only soften the environment, never alert, never log raw, and is always surfaced on the 'what it knows' page as a correctable belief.
- A vision-confidence → autonomy coupling: a vision-derived belief may only DRIVE an actuator (e.g. dim lights on detected wind-down) through the existing capability-gated act path (M5) at the LOW autonomy tier, and only after the belief is confirmed-once (the M7 ask-then-act ladder) — vision never gets a fast lane around the autonomy rails.

**Risks**

PRIVACY LEAK (his #1 fear, and vision is the worst offender): a faceprint, embedding, crop, or frame crossing the wire. Mitigation: raw stays on the Pi by construction; the ONLY thing that crosses is a declared-emittable normalized event; the M7 positive-schema guard (recursive, allowlist, rejects float-vector shapes at any depth) is a HARD prerequisite before R1 (faceprint) ships — vision is the reason that guard must be a real boundary, not a sanity check. The single emergency raw-egress path is the one audited exception and is owner-pre-authorised + logged.

FALSE-POSITIVE DISTRESS / NUISANCE ALERTS (becomes a nag AND erodes trust in the highest-stakes feature): a misread slump auto-dialing emergency services is catastrophic. Mitigation: the judgment ladder NEVER auto-acts on ambiguity (alert-him only); auto-egress requires UNMISTAKABLE + a no-response window; the whole ladder is tuned against the synthetic distress/ambiguous scenario corpus with a measured false-alarm rate before it can ever auto-call. Ship alert-him long before auto-call.

AFFECT IS PSEUDOSCIENCE IF OVERCLAIMED (mood/emotion read from posture is noisy; a confident wrong 'you seem angry' destroys the soul-feeling of 'it knows me'). Mitigation: R3 emits a BAND with a calibrated abstain default, only ever SOFTENS the environment (never alerts), is always a correctable belief on the trust page, and is gated behind R2 (bodily presence) proving reliable first. Deception/intent (R5+) is named as a horizon and explicitly NOT built — refusing to overclaim is the mitigation.

OVER-COMPLEXITY / SECOND WIRING (his #4 fear; a bespoke vision pipeline that bypasses the spine): mitigation — vision is JUST a PerceptionSource emitting typed events; no new graph, no new guard, no vector DB. It rides M2's synthetic harness and M5's capability gate. If it can't be expressed as normalized events through assert_emittable, it doesn't ship.

MUM'S FLAT BOUNDARY VIOLATION (a permanent red line): a camera angle or a learned pattern that represents her space. Mitigation: only the front door + his upstairs entrance ever have a FOV; mum's flat is unrepresentable by absence (no glyph/zone) enforced at perception ingest AND in GIST's OFF-fence; welfare signals derive ONLY from her front-door rhythm, flag-to-him, never surveil.

UNRELIABILITY OF EDGE INFERENCE (Hailo model drift, lighting, occlusion silently degrading detection → a guardian that misses things): mitigation — R0 ships with a measured detection-quality signal surfaced in the morning note; every rung has a named acceptance test on recorded traces; a degraded camera is reported (like a quarantined tile, M11), never silently trusted.

**Roadmap contribution**

- V0 (gates on M2 synthetic harness): Build the VisionPerception adapter shell in perception/ as a PerceptionSource that emits R0 events (presence.* + a coarse familiar/unfamiliar bit) through assert_emittable; ship the synthetic vision-event scenario library (unknown_return, fall_then_no_recovery, calm_wind_down, mum_rhythm_off) so every later rung is CI-replayable with no camera. Acceptance: a recorded entrance trace drives the existing Security tile through build_daemon; mum-flat events are rejected at ingest.
- V1 (gates on M7 positive-schema guard — HARD prerequisite): Declare the full vision event vocabulary in the positive schema; add the property test that NO faceprint/embedding/crop/float-vector of any key name or depth can cross. Then ship R1 on-Pi returning-unknown faceprint reduced to a coarse {household|known|guest|unknown} label before any event. Acceptance: identity vectors never appear on bus/mesh; 'same unknown returned twice' fires from labels alone.
- V2 (gates on M10 trust tiers + the morning note): R2 on-Pi pose/activity events feed the pattern-of-life so the morning recap and anticipation get bodily ('you usually settle at the desk ~09:00'); surface a per-camera detection-quality signal in the dream note so a degraded camera is reported, not silently trusted. Acceptance: a pose-trace measurably sharpens at least one anticipation; degraded-detection scenario produces a morning-note line.
- V3 (gates on M5 capability path + M7 ask-then-act): Ship the per-person/guest privacy gate AT THE PI (one-tap suppress-at-source). Then R3 coarse affect BAND that may ONLY soften the environment via the capability-gated act path at the LOW autonomy tier, confirmed-once, always a correctable belief on the 'what it knows' page. Acceptance: a guest-privacy scenario emits zero vision events; an affect band can dim lights only through a granted capability and never raises an alert.
- V4 (gates on V3 reliability + a measured false-alarm rate + M8 confirm path): Ship the distress/medical judgment ladder — AMBIGUOUS→alert-him; UNMISTAKABLE+no-response-in-N-seconds→the single owner-pre-authorised emergency raw-still egress; mum welfare at her front door only. Acceptance: ambiguous-slump scenario NEVER auto-calls; unmistakable-fall+no-response scenario triggers the audited egress exactly once and is logged; measured false-alarm rate on the corpus is reported as a number before auto-call is enabled.
- V5+ (named horizon, NOT scheduled, NOT promised): deception/intent reading. Stays an explicit dream, built only if R0–R4 are reliable and the value is proven — honest non-promise is the deliverable here.

### 3.11 · Business & life-admin: KartenWerk (inventory, market-watch + deal alerts, orders/shipping, grading) and life-admin (parcels, bills, notes/second-brain, drafting) — plus the "texts as him, in his style" capability with a hard buy/sell approval gate.

> Homie does 90% of the business and life-admin labor — watch, price, draft, fill — and stops every irreversible or outbound action at one Outbox swipe surface, with buying, selling, and money permanently behind a fresh per-action yes.

**Refined vision**

This domain is where "saves real time/effort" (trust-earner #3) gets concrete. The unifying invention is the **Outbox**: every action that leaves the house — a marketplace listing, a price change, a buy, a reply text, a bill payment — is first a *draft* in a single reviewable queue, never a fired action. Homie does ALL the upstream work (watch, price, draft, fill the form) and stops at the gate. The owner's whole job is to swipe a card on his phone: Send / Edit / Skip. That is the productivity multiplier: 90% of the labor done, 100% of the irreversible decisions his.

KartenWerk — a subprocess-isolated tile per external host (`egress:cardmarket`, `egress:ebay`), so its network reach is enforced by the manifest, not trusted prose. It holds local inventory (SQLite: card, set, condition, qty, cost-basis, grading status, listing refs). It runs a polite, cached market-watch (read-only, pre-approved like weather/prices per the interview) and emits two alert kinds: BUY ("a steal — €X under 90-day median, here's the listing") and SELL ("your held NM Charizard just spiked +28%, list into it?"). Both alerts are *proposals* in the Outbox — buying/selling specifically ALWAYS needs approval (his hard rule), enforced at the act path: the KartenWerk tile cannot fire a buy/sell/list/price-change actuator without a fresh `confirm.response{yes}` carrying a money-grade risk tag. Orders/shipping is the high-volume win: pack-and-ship checklists, address/label drafts, "mark sold → decrement inventory → draft the dispatch note." Grading is a tracker + a deal-spotting heuristic (ungraded card whose graded comps clear the grading fee with margin → "worth submitting").

Life-admin — a second-brain tile that is deliberately plain: parcels (DHL tracking once the owner approves the login — a per-carrier OAuth/credential held encrypted, never meshed), bills/appointments as dated obligations that surface in the morning recap and DUE lane (reusing GIST's existing DUE machinery, not a new scheduler), and a notes capture that writes plain markdown the cortex can read. Drafting is the connective tissue: "reply to this", "draft the dispatch note", "answer mum about Sunday" — Homie writes it; it lands in the Outbox; he approves.

"Texts as him, in his style" — the headline capability, built in three earned tiers. (1) **Draft-only**: every message is composed and queued; nothing sends without an explicit per-message yes. (2) **Style learning**: a local style profile distilled from his own approved/edited outbox (cadence, sign-offs, his lowercase, his imperfections — learned from what he *sends*, never scraped). The edit-distance between draft and what he actually sends IS the training signal — pure retrieval/prompt-conditioning, no weight tuning. (3) **Earned trust per-recipient-class**: only after a track record may a *trivial, reversible* class (e.g. "got it, thanks" auto-acks to known contacts) send without per-message confirm — and even that stays revocable, logged, and shown in the morning recap. Money and buy/sell NEVER enter the earned-autonomy ladder: they are permanently gated, full stop.

Everything in this domain obeys the four guardrails: it batches into ONE morning digest + on-demand (never a nag); it keeps the owner's contacts/finances local and the network reach host-scoped; it has full timeline undo (every outbox item, sent or skipped, is a reversible ledger row); and it stays plain — one Outbox, one inventory DB, one notes folder, no policy DSL.

**Inventions**

- The Outbox: a single persistent, reviewable queue of outbound DRAFTS (listings, price changes, buys, texts, bill payments, dispatch notes). One swipe surface — Send / Edit / Skip — that 90% of the labor flows into and 100% of irreversible decisions stop at. Survives restart; every row is an undoable ledger entry.
- A money-grade risk tier on the consent gate: KartenWerk buy/sell/list/price-change actuators are refused at the act path unless accompanied by a fresh confirm.response{yes} tagged risk='money'. This tier is OUTSIDE the earned-autonomy ladder — it can never be auto-promoted, mirroring the act-map's never-autonomous lock class.
- KartenWerk inventory store: a stdlib sqlite3 schema (card, set, condition, qty, cost-basis, grading-status, listing-ref, marketplace-id) with cost-basis tracking so SELL alerts can show realized margin, not just price.
- Market-watch deal engine: cached, rate-limited, read-only polling of card-market prices (pre-approved online use) computing a per-card 90-day median + volatility; fires BUY (≥X% under median) and SELL (≥Y% spike on a held NM+ card) proposals into the Outbox. Thresholds owner-tunable, defaults conservative to avoid nagging.
- Grading deal-spotter: a heuristic flagging ungraded inventory whose graded comparables clear (grading fee + shipping) with a margin floor — 'worth submitting' proposals, never auto-submitted.
- His-style writer: a local style profile distilled from his own approved/edited outbox history (sign-offs, casing, length, emoji habits, characteristic imperfections), conditioning the draft prompt. The draft↔sent edit-distance is the learning signal — retrieval/prompt-level, zero weight tuning, consistent with the §6 learning thesis.
- Per-recipient-class send-trust ladder for texting: starts draft-only; a class may earn unattended send ONLY for trivial reversible acks after a clean track record; revocable, logged, shown in the morning recap; money/buy-sell permanently excluded.
- Obligation surfacing via GIST's existing DUE lane: bills/appointments/parcel-ETAs render into the morning recap and a <24h DUE injection — reusing the memory format's machinery instead of inventing a calendar engine.
- Encrypted per-service credential store (Cardmarket/eBay/DHL logins) on the LUKS volume, held only by the relevant isolated tile, never meshed, panic-wipeable with the rest of key material.

**Risks**

"BIGGEST: an unintended financial action — a wrong buy, a mispriced listing, an underpriced sale. Mitigation: buy/sell/price-change live behind a money-grade consent tier that is structurally outside the autonomy ladder and refused at the act path without a fresh per-action yes; every such action shows price + cost-basis + margin in the confirm prompt; and is a one-key-undoable ledger row (cancel listing / relist). This is the lock-class discipline (M5) applied to money.\n\nIMPERSONATION/REPUTATION: 'texts as him' sending something wrong or off-tone to a real person. Mitigation: draft-only by default; per-message confirm; the earned-send class is restricted to trivial reversible acks to known contacts and is revocable; the style profile is built ONLY from his own sent messages (never scraped contacts), so it leaks nobody else's data; every auto-sent message is logged and surfaced in the morning recap so a bad pattern is caught within a day.\n\nNAG: deal alerts + bill reminders are exactly the feature most likely to become the annoyance the owner fears most. Mitigation: ONE batched morning digest plus on-demand only; conservative default deal thresholds; suppression learning (if he skips a class of alert repeatedly, it goes quiet, same friction loop as lighting); never an interrupt for anything non-urgent.\n\nPRIVACY/EGRESS: marketplace + carrier APIs are the domain's only routine outbound traffic, and they carry credentials + (for shipping) addresses. Mitigation: each external integration is a subprocess-isolated tile with a host-scoped egress manifest (no wildcard reach); credentials encrypted, tile-local, never meshed, panic-wipeable; addresses/contacts never leave the box except in the specific approved API call.\n\nDATA INTEGRITY: inventory/cost-basis is real money state — a corrupted or lost DB is a real loss. Mitigation: the inventory SQLite sits under the existing layered/encrypted backup; every mutation is a logged, reversible ledger event; reconcile against marketplace order history rather than trusting local state blindly.\n\nMARKET-DATA TRUST: scraped/aggregated prices can be stale or manipulated (thin markets, outliers). Mitigation: median-not-mean + volatility gating + minimum-sample floor before any alert fires; show the comps behind every proposal so he verifies; treat web prices skeptically and cite the source, per the interview's research posture."

**Roadmap contribution**

- B0 — The Outbox core (a sitting): a persistent, restart-surviving draft queue with one consent-backed Send/Edit/Skip surface in the cockpit, each row a reversible ledger entry. Build this FIRST — it's the gate every later business/life action flows through, and it's plain. Acceptance: a queued draft cannot fire its action without a confirm.response{yes}; Skip and post-send undo are logged ledger rows that survive restart.
- B1 — Life-admin second-brain tile (days): plain notes capture (markdown the cortex reads), bills/appointments as dated obligations rendered into the morning recap via GIST's DUE lane, and a draft-message intent that lands in the Outbox. No network yet. This is the cheapest 'saves real effort' win and reuses existing recall/recap surfaces. Acceptance: a captured obligation appears in the next morning recap and in the <24h DUE injection.
- B2 — KartenWerk inventory + grading tracker (days): the sqlite3 inventory schema with cost-basis and grading status; voice/chat intents to add/move/mark-sold; mark-sold decrements qty and drafts a dispatch note into the Outbox. Offline, no marketplace yet. Acceptance: mark-sold updates inventory and produces a draftable dispatch note; inventory survives restart and rides the encrypted backup.
- B3 — Money-grade consent tier + buy/sell gate (days): extend the consent risk tags with a 'money' tier refused at the act path without a fresh yes, structurally outside the autonomy ladder; confirm prompts show price+cost-basis+margin. This must land BEFORE any marketplace write path is wired (same discipline as M5: never wire the open path and harden later). Acceptance: a forged or unconfirmed buy/sell/price-change actuator is refused; a confirmed one succeeds and is an undoable ledger row.
- B4 — KartenWerk market-watch + deal engine (weeks): a subprocess-isolated `egress:cardmarket` tile doing cached read-only price polling, per-card 90-day median + volatility, firing BUY/SELL/grading proposals into the Outbox (read-only, pre-approved). Deal thresholds owner-tunable; suppression-learning on skipped alert classes. Acceptance: a synthetic price drop produces exactly one BUY proposal (not an interrupt) with comps shown; repeated skips silence that class.
- B5 — Marketplace order/shipping write path (weeks): list / price-change / mark-dispatched via the marketplace API, each routed through B3's money gate and the Outbox; reconcile local inventory against fetched order history. Acceptance: a list action requires money-grade confirm, shows margin, appears as an undoable ledger row, and local inventory matches the marketplace after reconcile.
- B6 — Parcels + carrier integration (days): an approved, encrypted per-carrier credential (DHL) in an isolated `egress:dhl` tile; parcel ETAs render into the morning recap/DUE. Acceptance: a tracked parcel's ETA shows in the morning recap; the credential is tile-local, encrypted, and panic-wipeable.
- B7 — His-style writer + draft-only texting (weeks): distill a local style profile from his approved/edited Outbox history; condition message drafts on it; everything draft-only through the Outbox. Acceptance: drafts measurably shift toward his style (lower draft↔sent edit-distance over time) using only his own sent history; no message sends without per-message confirm.
- B8 — Earned per-recipient-class send-trust (weeks, last): allow ONLY trivial reversible acks to known contacts to send without per-message confirm after a clean track record; revocable; every auto-send logged and surfaced in the morning recap; money/buy-sell permanently excluded. Acceptance: a class earns unattended ack only after N clean confirms, a single bad outcome revokes it, and every auto-sent message appears in the recap.

### 3.12 · Network & cyber-defense — "Homie lives in the network as much as the PC": network-aware presence/identity, device inventory + unknown-device flagging, and active cyber-defense (block/quarantine/isolate) as a guarded-but-not-noisy perimeter.

> Make the home LAN a calm, guarded body Homie inhabits — learn every device as pattern-of-life, flag only the genuinely unknown, and gate block/quarantine/isolate behind the capability+undo rails — by applying 'presence detection to the wire,' reusing the one tested organism rather than building an IDS.

**Refined vision**

Homie treats the home LAN as a second body it inhabits. Three concrete layers, each earning the next, all reusing the existing organism (build_daemon graph, Perceive intake seam, the (topic,zone) pattern-of-life model, the capability-gated act path, AnchorVoice for honest replies).

LAYER 1 — KNOW THE NETWORK (passive, ships first, zero blast radius). A NetSense source feeds the existing Perceive.run seam, NOT a new pipeline. It learns the LAN's pattern of life exactly like presence: every device is a stable identity (MAC + DHCP fingerprint + vendor OUI), and each becomes a (topic="net.device.seen", zone=device-id) entry in the SAME PatternModel the home already uses. So "this MAC normally appears 18:00–23:00" is learned by the proven histogram, not new code. Source of truth is Home Assistant's existing device_tracker / router integration first (it already does ARP/DHCP scanning); a tiny stdlib ARP-table/`ip neigh` reader on the always-on box is the fallback. The owner's phone is a first-class identity here, fusing with HA presence so "his phone on the network" becomes one more presence signal among door/motion/face (round 3: presence is one signal among several). Output: a "Devices Homie knows" page (the network half of the "what it knows" page the owner ranked as first-win), plain language: "your phone, mum's phone, the Dirigera hub, 3 IKEA bulbs, the TV, two laptops — all regulars."

LAYER 2 — FLAG THE UNKNOWN (quiet, behavior-not-blocklist). A new "netguard" tile, sibling to the security tile and built the same way: it subscribes to net.device.* and consults ctx.recall against the pattern of life. A device that is genuinely novel for this home, or a known device behaving abnormally (a bulb suddenly talking to the internet, a new MAC at 3am), emits net.alert — which flows through the EXACT same capture→alert→your-decision path as physical intrusion (round 5). Crucially it inherits the M3 calibrated-surprise + token-budget governance, so it is rare-relative-to-THIS-home, never a global threshold, and can never become a nag (the owner's #1 thing to prevent). Default behavior matches the entrances rule: log quietly, speak only when genuinely odd. A guest's phone joining once = silent log; an unrecognized device port-scanning the LAN at 3am = speak up.

LAYER 3 — ACTIVE DEFENSE (gated like the lock; the most-guarded actuator class, longest leash). block/quarantine/isolate are modeled as actuators behind the M5 capability gate and the autonomy ladder. They are irreversible-adjacent (round 9: irreversible guarded longest), so they DEFAULT to propose-not-act: "an unknown device is scanning the LAN — quarantine it?" with one-tap yes, and an always-available undo (un-quarantine) in the Friction Ledger. Mechanism is deliberately boring and reliable: the always-on box already sits on the network; quarantine = move the offender to an isolation VLAN / drop it via the router's firewall API (HA can already call many routers; OpenWRT/firewalld/nftables as the stdlib fallback), NOT custom packet wizardry. Two narrow exceptions may act-then-tell autonomously (full-deterrence, round 5): an active outbound exfiltration from a Homie machine, and a device actively attacking another (these are unmistakable, like the "bleeding out" emergency-call test). Everything else proposes. Every block is logged, reversible, and explained.

THE RECONCILED SELF-EVOLUTION RAIL (my domain's piece of the central tension): the network is the one place "bold, always-latest, broad-internet" updates physically enter the home. So the perimeter is also the update gate. Homie's own machines get an egress-allowlist posture: in normal operation they reach only the vetted update channel + the owner's pre-approved online uses (weather, card prices); a self-upgrade that wants to pull from the broad internet must pass THROUGH this guard, which logs every destination. This is how aggressive self-improvement and strong rails coexist: the code may rewrite itself boldly, but its network reach is allowlisted and every byte out is recorded and capped — bold inside a guarded box.

What this is NOT (cutting over-complexity, the owner's explicit fear): no IDS/Suricata/Zeek stack, no packet capture, no SIEM, no per-flow ML. The whole thing reuses one pattern-of-life model + one alert path + one capability gate the home already has. It is "presence detection, applied to the wire."

**Inventions**

- NetSense — a passive network-presence source implementing the existing Perceive.run intake seam: enumerates LAN devices via HA device_tracker first / stdlib `ip neigh` + ARP-table + DHCP-lease parsing as fallback, normalizes each to a stable identity (MAC + OUI vendor + DHCP-hostname fingerprint), and emits net.device.seen/new/gone events. Crucially NOT a new pipeline — it feeds the same bus the camera/door does.
- Device-identity fusion — a small canonical layer (sibling to ha_canonical) that maps a churning set of MACs/IPs/randomized-MACs to one durable 'device' identity, and ties the owner's phone identity to the existing presence signal so 'his phone on the LAN' is one more layer of who's-home, not a separate system.
- netguard tile — a stdlib tile mirroring the security tile: consults the (topic,zone) pattern-of-life via ctx.recall, emits net.alert for novel/abnormal devices, inherits M3 calibrated-surprise + token budget so it can never nag. Default: log-quietly, speak-only-if-odd.
- Network actuators behind the M5 capability gate — block(device), quarantine(device→isolation VLAN), isolate(machine), each declared in a manifest with priority, default propose-not-act, with an inverse act (un-block/un-quarantine) wired for one-key undo. Two narrow autonomous exceptions (active exfiltration from a Homie box; a device actively attacking another) act-then-tell.
- Router/firewall control adapter behind a HomeClient-style seam — call_service to HA-supported routers first; OpenWRT/nftables/firewalld as the stdlib-shellout fallback. One seam, swappable, so the dangerous capability has exactly one tested implementation path (the build_daemon discipline applied to the perimeter).
- Egress-allowlist posture for Homie's own machines — the perimeter doubles as the self-upgrade gate: normal egress is allowlisted to the vetted update channel + pre-approved online uses; broad-internet self-upgrade pulls pass through it and every destination is logged and capped. This is the concrete reconciliation of 'bold self-improvement' with 'strong rails' on the network side.
- The 'Devices Homie knows' page — the network half of the 'what it knows' page: a plain-language, correctable inventory (this is your phone / mum's phone / the hub / regulars), reusing the same read-only cockpit projection M10 builds for pattern-of-life. Owner can name a device or mark it trusted, which teaches the pattern model.
- Sealed-perimeter report — an extension of M10's 'Sealed Network' report generated from the SAME allowlist the egress guard enforces, so the displayed promise ('only these destinations, only these devices may talk out') is byte-identical to runtime behavior — no drift between claim and enforcement.

**Risks**

"NAGGING / false positives (the owner's #1 fear): a noisy network guard that cries wolf at every guest phone or randomized-MAC is worse than none. Mitigate by reusing the M3 calibrated-per-home surprise score + token budget and the 'log-quietly, speak-only-if-odd' default — the guard must be CALM by construction, tuned against this home's own device churn, not a global rule. Randomized MAC rotation (modern phones) is the specific trap: dampen by fingerprinting on DHCP/vendor/behavior, not MAC alone, and treating 'new unknown' as silent-log until it behaves oddly. | SELF-LOCKOUT / fragility (fear #2, reliability is the #1 trust earner): an active-defense bug that quarantines the owner's phone, the Dirigera hub, or a Homie machine itself bricks the home. Mitigate: active defense defaults to PROPOSE-not-act; a hard never-touch allowlist (owner phone, hub, all Homie machines, mum's devices) that block/quarantine can NEVER target — enforced at the actuator, the same way the act-map is the hard boundary; every action reversible with one-key undo; the two autonomous exceptions are narrow and self-excluding. Test the lockout: a regression that a forged or buggy quarantine of a never-touch device is refused. | PRIVACY LEAK (fear #3): network metadata (who-talks-to-what) is sensitive, and the 3-machine topology means it could cross a wire. Mitigate: device identities are LOCAL-only, never meshed (same rule as faceprints); the M7 positive-schema guard covers net.* topics; mum's devices are in the never-represent set exactly like her flat — Homie may know the front door's network but never inventory or watch her flat's devices. | OVER-COMPLEXITY (fear #4): the gravitational pull toward a full IDS/packet-capture/SIEM. Mitigate by HARD anti-goal: no Suricata/Zeek/pcap/per-flow ML, ever, until a real threat the simple model misses names it. The entire design is 'presence detection applied to the wire' — one pattern model, one alert path, one capability gate, all already built and tested. | ROUTER DEPENDENCY: active defense needs a controllable router/firewall; consumer routers vary wildly and may not expose an API. Mitigate: degrade gracefully — if no enforcement path exists, the guard still DETECTS and ALERTS (Layer 1+2 work everywhere); active defense (Layer 3) is gated behind 'a supported enforcement seam is present', and proposes manual steps otherwise rather than failing silently."

### 3.13 · Scope discipline & simplicity — the smallest Homie that delivers the soul (knows-him + effortlessness) without becoming a cathedral

> Treat the 100-question interview as a 10-year want-map, not a backlog: the soul is already 80% built — ship the morning note, the what-I-know page, one-key undo, and lights/climate autonomy, shelve the GIST crypto-codec and the three-node mesh, split self-evolution into free-prompt-drift vs owner-signed-releases, and make every future ADD name a CUT.

**Refined vision**

The soul is two felt promises: "it knows me" and "things quietly take care of themselves." Everything else is decoration the owner has not lived with yet. The smallest system that delivers the soul is already 80% built — M0–M6 plus the HA hand. What remains to reach the SOUL is small: the morning dream note (M10's note half), the "what Homie knows" page (a read-only render of remember.py), one-key undo (M8), and the lighting/climate autonomy rung he already trusts. That is the whole product. Ship THAT, live with it for a month, and let the lived gaps — not the interview's wish-list — authorize the next thing.

The contrarian re-read of the interview: the owner said "everything," but he also named OVER-COMPLEXITY as one of four hard guardrails and EFFORTLESSNESS as the single feeling he wants. Those two facts outrank the feature list. A cathedral is not effortless to its builder either — a solo SSH-from-phone owner cannot operate a system with a DP-budgeted HMAC'd integer memory codec, active cyber-defense, self-rewriting code, and camera intent-reading. Each of those is a standing liability he must understand, trust, and debug at 2am. The discipline: the interview is a 10-YEAR want-map, not a backlog. Re-rank it by "does this serve knows-me-or-effortless THIS QUARTER," and almost everything ambitious falls below the line.

Concrete cut list, by how dangerous the complexity is:

(1) GIST full format — SHELVE the codec, KEEP the distillation. Two prior reviews already converged here and were overruled by a 21-agent council into an integer codec with varint zigzag-delta coding, EW moments, symmetric Beta, two-rate change-point, DP noise budgets, HKDF, and an HMAC over canonical integer bytes. This is the single clearest cathedral in the tree. The SOUL needs exactly: nightly distill the day's events into a few hundred bytes of decayed per-(topic,zone,daytype) stats + a plain-English brief the LLM reads + a readable "what I know" render. remember.py already IS the decayed stat store. Build = the 3 honest stat fixes (present_days for real P≤1, β-on-no-show so stopped routines fade, day-type bucket) + a prose renderer over remember.py's existing model. Drop the codec, HMAC, DP, varint, two-rate, anomaly reservoir entirely. They sign and protect a thing that never leaves the box. LUKS already covers at-rest. A determined adversary with the password reads everything anyway (the doc admits this) — so the in-Python crypto buys nothing the disk doesn't.

(2) Self-rewriting code from the broad internet — REFUSE the dangerous half, KEEP the safe half. Reconcile the tension by SPLITTING it: Homie may freely (a) propose automations, (b) adapt its PROMPT/personality notes (data, instantly reversible, zero blast radius), and (c) pull the OWNER'S OWN signed git tags through the existing update.py health-gate + NixOS atomic rollback. It may NOT pull arbitrary internet code, and it may NOT author and merge its own source unattended. "Self-coding with tests+rollback" sounds bold but the test suite is written by the same system proposing the change — a model that rewrites its own grader is the textbook unsafe loop, and it serves NEITHER knows-me nor effortless. The bold feeling the owner wants ("always latest") is fully delivered by signed-tag + atomic rollback. Keep self-evolution as prompt/preference drift + owner-signed releases; that is bold AND has rails, no contradiction.

(3) Active cyber-defense / network guardian — DEFER to ALERT-ONLY. "Block/quarantine/isolate" autonomously is a foot-gun that will eventually lock the owner out of his own network or break mum's connectivity, violating effortlessness and the never-a-nag rule in one stroke. Smallest soul-serving version: detect-and-notify ("a new device joined; unusual outbound from the TV"). Earn the autonomous-block rung the same way lights earned theirs — after months of correct alerts. Active defense is the most-guarded autonomy class (irreversible-ish, household-affecting), so by the owner's OWN ladder it comes last, not in the guardian-pillar first cut.

(4) Camera human-reading (mood→distress→intent→deception) — KEEP AT NORTH-STAR, not roadmap. The owner himself called it "years out." Near-term camera = presence + stranger-flag at the two watched entrances only (already decided). Deception-cue reading is a research programme, not a feature, and a half-working version is creepy and wrong — exactly the "janky gesture" failure mode he rejected. One line in the vision, zero lines in the next year's code.

(5) The three-machine mesh — the masterplan's own panel already voted to collapse the brain to ONE node and the owner re-opened it to keep three. Honor the owner BUT note the cost honestly: keeping three machines talking re-introduces the entire C3/C6/partition bug class and forces the positive-schema cross-node guard to be a real security boundary (more work) instead of a sanity check. Contrarian recommendation: run single-brain-node in PRACTICE now (Pi = camera edge only, one always-on box = HA+brain+LLM), keep the MeshTransport seam so three-node is a config flag if a second location ever appears. Build mesh transport only when a tripwire fires. Do not build deploy/mesh/ now.

(6) Emergency auto-calling services — KEEP, but the smallest version: alert-the-owner is the default path; auto-call only on the narrowest unmistakable trigger with a confirm-window. This is already the decided shape; just resist any urge to broaden the judgment model. The liability of a false auto-call to police is enormous; keep the trigger boring.

The smallest system that delivers the soul, stated as a product: a reliable always-on box that drives his lights/climate, hears his corrections, distills each night into a plain morning note and a correctable "what I know" page, lets him undo anything in one key, flags strangers at two doors, and alerts (not acts) on network/emergency anomalies. Everything else — KartenWerk automation, parcel logins, market deal-bots, DP memory, self-coding, intent-vision, autonomous cyber-defense — is a SEPARATE tile or a SEPARATE year, added one at a time only after the soul is lived-in and the reliability trust is banked.

**Inventions**

- A written 'Complexity Budget' doc (docs/SCOPE.md): a hard rule that no new subsystem ships until it serves knows-me-or-effortless THIS quarter AND the owner can operate it from an SSH phone session. Every council that votes to ADD must name what it CUTS. This is the structural defense against the 21-agent-council ratchet that turned 'go lean' into an HMAC codec.
- A 'one-tile-at-a-time' gate for the business/admin wishes: KartenWerk, parcels, bills, market-watch each become an independent opt-in tile added only after the soul ships — never bundled into the core daemon. Invent the boundary now (tiles stay off the critical path), build the tiles later on demand.
- A 'lived-gap log': instead of building from the interview, ship the soul and keep a running note of what the owner actually reaches for and it can't do. The NEXT roadmap is authored from that log, not from the want-map. This inverts feature-selection from aspiration to evidence — the same discipline the masterplan already applies to fine-tuning ('let evidence authorize a trainer, or kill it').
- A self-evolution SPLIT spec: a one-page rule separating (a) prompt/preference drift = free + instant-undo, from (b) source/system upgrades = owner-signed-tag + health-gate + atomic-rollback only. No third category. This resolves the interview's bold-vs-safe tension by construction instead of by judgment.

**Risks**

"The biggest risk is NOT a missing feature — it is the council/brainstorm machinery itself becoming the complexity engine. Evidence is in the tree: two reviews said 'GIST lean = 3 stat fixes + plain notes,' and a 21-agent council overrode them into an integer codec with DP, HMAC, varint coding, and a 9-stage build plan for a file that never leaves an already-encrypted disk. Second risk: the interview's 'everything' read literally produces a system the solo owner cannot operate or trust — directly tripping his over-complexity AND effortlessness guardrails. Third risk: the dangerous half of self-evolution (broad-internet code pull, self-authored merges) is a genuine security hole that serves neither soul promise; mitigation is the prompt-vs-signed-release split — keep the bold feeling, drop the unsafe mechanism. Fourth risk: autonomous cyber-defense and broadened emergency-calling create irreversible, household-affecting actions that violate the owner's own autonomy-ladder (most-guarded-longest); mitigation is alert-only until months of correct alerts earn the action rung. Residual honestly stated: cutting GIST-crypto and three-node-mesh now means IF a second resident or location ever needs real cross-node privacy isolation, that work returns — but the MeshTransport seam and the shelved GIST spec keep both reversible at config/spec level, so deferral costs a flag-flip and a doc, not a rewrite."
